1 The book of the genealogy of JesusYehoshua (Yehoshua, 'Yahweh is salvation') Christ,† the son of DavidDawid (Dawid, 'Beloved'), the son of AbrahamAvraham (Avraham, 'Father of many nations').

2 Abraham became the father of IsaacYitzchaq (Yitzchaq, 'He laughs'). Isaac became the father of JacobYaacob (Yaacob, 'Heel-grabber / supplanter'). Jacob became the father of JudahYahudah (Yahudah, 'Praise of Yahweh') and his brothers. 3 Judah became the father of Perez and Zerah by Tamar ('Palm tree'). Perez became the father of Hezron. Hezron became the father of Ram. 4 Ram became the father of Amminadab. Amminadab became the father of Nahshon. Nahshon became the father of Salmon. 5 Salmon became the father of Boaz ('Strength is in him') by RahabRachav (Rachav, 'Wide / broad'). Boaz became the father of Obed by RuthRut (Rut, 'Friend / companion'). Obed became the father of JesseYishai (Yishai, 'Gift / wealthy'). 6 Jesse became the father of King David. David the king‡ became the father of SolomonShelomoh (Shelomoh, 'Peace / peaceful') by her who had been UriahUriyahu (Uriyahu, 'Yahweh is my light')’s wife. 7 Solomon became the father of RehoboamRechav'am (Rechav'am, 'The people are enlarged'). Rehoboam became the father of Abijah. Abijah became the father of Asa ('Healer / physician'). 8 Asa became the father of JehoshaphatYehoshaphat (Yehoshaphat, 'Yahweh has judged'). Jehoshaphat became the father of Joram. Joram became the father of UzziahUzziyahu (Uzziyahu, 'Yahweh is my strength'). 9 Uzziah became the father of JothamYotam (Yotam, 'Yahweh is perfect'). Jotham became the father of AhazAchaz (Achaz, 'He has grasped'). Ahaz became the father of HezekiahChizqiyahu (Chizqiyahu, 'Yahweh strengthens'). 10 Hezekiah became the father of ManassehMenasheh (Menasheh, 'Causing to forget'). Manasseh became the father of Amon. Amon became the father of JosiahYoshiyahu (Yoshiyahu, 'Yahweh heals / Yahweh supports'). 11 Josiah became the father of Jechoniah and his brothers at the time of the exile to BabylonBavel (Bavel, 'Gate of God / confusion').

12 After the exile to Babylon, Jechoniah became the father of Shealtiel. Shealtiel became the father of Zerubbabel. 13 Zerubbabel became the father of Abiud. Abiud became the father of Eliakim. Eliakim became the father of Azor. 14 Azor became the father of ZadokTsadoq (Tsadoq, 'Righteous'). Zadok became the father of Achim. Achim became the father of Eliud. 15 Eliud became the father of Eleazar. Eleazar became the father of Matthan. Matthan became the father of Jacob. 16 Jacob became the father of JosephYosef (Yosef, 'He will add'), the husband of MaryMiriam (Miriam, 'Possibly bitter, rebellion, beloved, or wished-for child'), from whom was born Jesus,§ who is called Christ.

17 So all the generations from Abraham to David are fourteen generations; from David to the exile to Babylon fourteen generations; and from the carrying away to Babylon to the Christ, fourteen generations.

18 Now the birth of Jesus Christ was like this: After his mother, Mary, was engaged to Joseph, before they came together, she was found pregnant by the Holy Spirit. 19 Joseph, her husband, being a righteous man, and not willing to make her a public example, intended to put her away secretly. 20 But when he thought about these things, behold,† an angel of the Lord appeared to him in a dream, saying, “Joseph, son of David, don’t be afraid to take to yourself Mary as your wife, for that which is conceived in her is of the Holy Spirit. 21 She shall give birth to a son. You shall name him Jesus,‡ for it is he who shall save his people from their sins.”

22 Now all this has happened that it might be fulfilled which was spoken by the Lord through the prophet, saying,

23 “Behold, the virgin shall be with child,

and shall give birth to a son.

They shall call his name Immanuel,”

which is, being interpreted, “God with us.”*

24 Joseph arose from his sleep, and did as the angel of the Lord commanded him, and took his wife to himself; 25 and didn’t know her sexually until she had given birth to her firstborn son. He named him Jesus.

† 1:1 Messiah (Hebrew) and Christ (Greek) both mean “Anointed One”

‡ 1:6 NU omits “the king”.

§ 1:16 “Jesus” means “Salvation”.

† 1:20 “Behold”, from “ἰδοὺ”, means look at, take notice, observe, see, or gaze at. It is often used as an interjection.

‡ 1:21 “Jesus” means “Salvation”.
